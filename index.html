About Me
Hi! I'm Sloan - a rocketry enthusiast, teaching assistant at Synthesis, and Formula One fanatic. Although seemingly unrelated, if I could summarize my interests in one word, it would be advancement. I have always enjoyed learning about where things are going, and what I can do now. I am a strong believer in the future of space exploration, and the improvement of education. Formula One intersts me because it is a window into where general engineering technology will be in 5, 10 or 20 years.

It is also the cross section between engineering and competition. Having played competitive basketball for 8 years, I like playing pick-up games, but more broadly, enjoy the challenge and collaboration sports bring. I am also a first year engineering student at the University of British Colombia, in Vancouver, Canada where I have continued to pursue my interest in rocketry on the UBC Rocket team.

Testing the Efficiency of Rocket Nose Cones

I wrote my design technology extended essay on the aerodynamics of rocket nose cones. Through this paper I assess six different types of nose cones at seven different fineness ratios. They are compared based on their drag coefficient and apogee when attached to a standard rocket.
